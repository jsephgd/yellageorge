The one that got away.

The Case of Yella George

Imagine living in a small, dainty, beautiful, rural town deep in the north Florida countryside. Now imagine that small town being haunted by the disappearance of 4 women. This was the only thing being talked about in Hamilton county 60 some odd years ago. Rachael Brooks, Carly Daysaliba, Morgan Adams and Sarabeth Adams (sisters). Of the missing women, Rachael Brooks was the only one to turn up alive. Some believe her story, some believe it was a lie to cover herself. Her father, a long time local and county commissioner, is believed to have been the one who stopped the murders or as some have said in hush terms, he may have scared the real killer into stopping his rampage. At that time, interracial tension was high in the southern communities, integration with the school systems had began in those areas and so had romantic relationships. Many leaders in these communities did not like their children mixing races romantically. Not long after Rachael “escaped” George Lawrence’s residence, George, also referred to as Yella George, was found hanging on his front porch. There was never an investigation into his death, but many believe he was a victim of a racially motivated killing. However, after his death, no more women went missing. The unfortunate side to this story is, because of the odd nature of George’s death, and an investigation never taking place, the search for the three missing women was also halted.
